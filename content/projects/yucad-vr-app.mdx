## Overview

Created a mobile application enabling users to view their home designs stored in YUCAD accounts through multiple perspectives: 2D floorplans, 3D interactive models, top-down views, and immersive VR walkthroughs. The app bridged design creation and visualization, enhancing decision-making and design sharing across platforms and devices.

## The Challenge

Desktop design tools limited when and where users could review and share their designs. Construction professionals and homeowners needed mobile access to present designs to clients and make decisions on-site. The challenge was to deliver a high-quality 3D visualization experience within the constraints of mobile hardware while supporting both Android and iOS platforms.

## Technical Approach

Developed a cross-platform mobile architecture that optimized 3D rendering for mobile GPUs while maintaining visual quality. Implemented multiple viewing modes with seamless transitions between 2D and 3D representations of the same designs.

## Key Implementations

- **Cross-Platform Architecture:** Built unified codebase supporting both Android and iOS, with platform-specific optimizations for each ecosystem's GPU requirements and certification requirements.

- **Multiple Visualization Modes:** Implemented 2D floorplan views, interactive 3D models with orbit controls, top-down overview perspectives, and immersive VR walkthrough experiences.

- **VR Walkthrough Integration:** Created room-scale VR experiences that allowed users to physically walk through their designs using Google Cardboard or similar mobile VR headsets.

- **Design Synchronization:** Implemented cloud-based design synchronization, ensuring users always access the latest versions of their designs across devices and platforms.

- **Performance Optimization:** Developed dynamic quality scaling based on device capability, ensuring smooth performance on older devices while maximizing visual quality on flagship phones.

## Outcome

The mobile app expanded the YUCAD ecosystem's accessibility, enabling designers and clients to review and approve designs from anywhere. Mobile engagement increased significantly, and the VR walkthrough feature became a popular sales tool for design presentations.
