## Overview

Beyond Guns was developed as part of a master's thesis, focused on evaluating and comparing the immersive qualities of First-Person and Third-Person perspectives in shooter games. The research explored how camera perspective influences user experience through gameplay factors such as control fidelity, narrative engagement, and physical discomfort.

## The Challenge

Understanding immersion in games requires controlled experimentation that isolates specific design variables while maintaining engaging experiences. The challenge was to create two equivalent gameplay experiences that differed only in camera perspective, enabling meaningful comparative analysis.

## Technical Approach

Built parallel gameplay prototypes maintaining identical mechanics to isolate perspective as the only variable. Conducted structured user testing sessions with quantitative and qualitative data collection.

## Key Implementations

- **Immersion-Focused Gameplay Design:** Built two parallel gameplay prototypes — one in first-person, the other in third-person — while maintaining identical mechanics to isolate perspective as the only variable affecting immersion.

- **Comparative Analysis Metrics:** Analyzed player responses across key dimensions: camera control, weapon feedback, environmental awareness, and character connection, developing validated survey instruments.

- **Motion Sickness Observation:** Evaluated physical discomfort and disorientation tendencies in FPS mode versus TPS, based on structured user testing and observation protocols.

- **Data Collection & Reporting:** Conducted comprehensive user testing sessions and recorded subjective and objective feedback to assess player preference and immersion factors across demographic groups.

- **Literature Review & Framework:** Established theoretical grounding through review of existing immersion research, adapting established frameworks for the specific context of camera perspective comparison.

## Outcome

The thesis received distinction from the examination committee. The research methodology and survey instruments developed were subsequently adopted for use in other UX studies within the game design program, contributing to the program's empirical research capabilities.
