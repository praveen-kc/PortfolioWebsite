## Overview

Malfunky is a third-person 3D platformer created by a team of 11, including programmers, artists, designers, and audio specialists. Developed for Xbox 360 and PC, the game follows the journey of a fragmented, sentient robot navigating a hazardous factory. Players must reunite its three separated components, each with unique abilities, to solve puzzles and escape the facility.

## The Challenge

Team-based game development requires coordination across disciplines with different priorities and skill levels. Academic timelines create pressure to deliver complete, polished experiences. The challenge was to manage a large team while producing a coherent, playable game within semester constraints.

## Technical Approach

Contributed as one of five programmers in a diverse development team. Focused on core platformer mechanics and console-specific optimizations while collaborating with designers and artists for puzzle integration.

## Key Implementations

- **Cross-Disciplinary Collaboration:** Contributed as one of five programmers in a diverse development team, collaborating closely with designers and artists to align gameplay mechanics and visual storytelling.

- **Platformer Core Mechanics:** Helped implement movement logic, camera systems, and platforming interactions tailored for controller-based play on Xbox 360, including physics tuning for responsive feel.

- **Puzzle Integration:** Supported development of object-switching mechanics and character-specific puzzle-solving based on each robot part's abilities, working with level designers to implement their visions.

- **Multi-Platform Deployment:** Optimized builds and inputs for both Xbox 360 and PC platforms, ensuring smooth user experience across target devices with different input paradigms.

- **Audio Integration:** Coordinated with audio specialists to integrate dynamic sound effects and music that responded to gameplay state.

## Outcome

The game was successfully presented at the university showcase event, receiving positive feedback from peers and faculty. The project's production documentation became a case study for subsequent cohorts, illustrating effective team coordination strategies for game development projects.
